    \section*{An Overview of the Strategy}

    Use Annex XII from Belgian NIR from 2024 and 2025 to compute the share of emissions in each IPCC category not covered by the EUETS.

    Assume that this share is fixed between 2005 and 2023 (as far as emissions data go). What does this assumption imply? It implies that there is no reallocation between treated and non-treated firms within sector.

    That's exactly the kind of thing we would like to check if it's true or not.

    Data on EUETS goes back to 2025. But the \textit{levels} are off compared to numbers from Annex XII documentation.

    Question: do we have evidence of changes between EUTL/total emissions ratio by sector?

    Three data sources:
    1. total emissions by IPCC category
    2. EUETS emissions by IPCC category from EUTL
    3. EUETS emissions by IPCC category from Annexx XII

    Assume EUTL/Annex XII emissions by sector is constant over time (miss-classification is constant).

    This implies looking at EUTL/(total emissiions from IPCC guidelines) tells us about EUETS Annex XII/(total emissiions from IPCC guidelines) for years other than 2024/25.

    If EUTL/(total emissiions from IPCC guidelines) is constant over time, then conclude that ratio EUETS Annex XII/(total emissions from IPCC guidelines) is constant over time and simply use this ratio to impute emissions accordingly.

    If EUTL/(total emissiions from IPCC guidelines) is not constant over time, then suggestive evidence of domestic leakage and see what to do with it.
